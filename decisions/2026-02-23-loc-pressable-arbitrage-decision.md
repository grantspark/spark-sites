# LOC + Pressable Hosting Arbitrage — Proceed Despite 6-Month Delay

## Situation

Spark is paying $2,000/month to Flywheel for hosting and carrying $27,000 in credit card debt (~28% APR). An OnDeck business line of credit ($40K limit, 52.4% APR, 24-month amortization) is available. The original plan was to borrow $10K, pay Pressable annually ($8K), eliminate Flywheel, apply $2K to cards, and repay the LOC when a $10K Pressable deposit arrived quickly.

**New information:** The Pressable $10K deposit is delayed 6 months.

## Decision

Proceed with the arbitrage play. The 6-month delay does not break the strategy.

## Why

- Eliminating $2,000/month Flywheel saves $12,000 over 6 months
- LOC carry cost for 6 months is ~$2,413 in interest — a 5:1 return on carry cost
- Monthly cash flow improves by ~$1,322 immediately
- When the $10K arrives at month 6, it wipes the LOC (~$7,985) with ~$2,015 surplus to cards
- Even the most aggressive CC paydown scenario ($1,750/mo to cards) still costs $322/month less than doing nothing

## Conditions for Success

1. **Redirect freed cash to debt.** If the ~$1,322/month improvement gets absorbed into operations, the arbitrage benefit evaporates.
2. **Do not draw casually on the LOC.** The $10K draw is strategic. Additional draws at 52.4% APR compound fast.
3. **Pressable deposit must actually arrive at month 6.** If it slips further, the carry cost grows but the arbitrage still holds — just less efficiently.

## What Kills This Plan

- Revenue drops and the $738 LOC payment becomes a strain
- Freed cash gets spent instead of applied to debt
- Casual additional LOC draws turn a tactical tool into structural high-interest debt
